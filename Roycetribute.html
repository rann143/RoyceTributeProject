- "The idea of Jiu-Jitsu is to give the little guy a chance to beat the big guy."

Royce Gracie is a Legendary Jiu-Jitsu Practitioner

Trained by his father, Royce Gracie began competing in Jiu-Jitsu tournaments since he was 8 years old. He grew up to become one of Jiu-Jitsu's most prominent and influential figures. Using techniques from this relatively new martial art, he became a three-time UFC champion. Throughout his life, he has helped grow awareness of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and MMA. In 2003, he became the first fighter to be inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame.

Timeline

1966
Royce Gracie is born in Rio de Janeiro to Grand Master, Helio Gracie
1975
Royce Gracie begins competing in tournaments
1984
Royce earns his black belt in Jiu Jitsu
1989
Establishes the Gracie Jiu-Jitsu Academy with his brother, Rorion in Torrance, California
1993
Begins his career as an MMA defeating 3 opponents at the first UFC championship in Denver, Colorado
2000
Fights with Kazushi Sakuraba in PRIDE Grand PRIX, going on to hold the record for longest MMA match - 90 minutes
2003
Royce is the first person to be inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame alongside Ken Shamrock, another UFC competitor
2014
Named one of the 50 Greatest Athletes in the World by Sports Illustrated
Present
Continues to live in Southern California with his family. He continues to train Jiu-Jitsu.
